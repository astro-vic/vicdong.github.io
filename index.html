NSF Graduate Research Fellow at CIERA, Northwestern University

Howdy! I am an NSF Graduate Research Fellow at CIERA, Northwestern University, working with Dr. Wen-fai Fong. I'm broadly interested in understanding the origin and evolution of cosmic transients and their environments. My graduate studies focus on uncovering the physical origins of fast radio bursts using multi-wavelength observations of their local and global environments.

I am also an active member of scientific collaborations including the Fast and Fortunate for FRB Follow-up (F4) collaboration and CHIME/FRB. When I'm not in the office, I enjoy running, hiking, visiting national parks, and reading :)

Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs)

Probing the origins of FRBs with multi-wavelength observations (radio, optical, X-ray) and detailed studies of their local and host-galaxy environments.

Topics: burst energetics • local environments • host demographics • DM/rotation measures
